La Recoleta Cemetery

La Recoleta Cemetery is a cemetery located in the Recoleta neighborhood of Buenos Aires, Argentina. It contains the graves of notable people, including Eva Perón and the founder of the Argentine Navy. It is a popular landmark with tourists, and when I visited the site, there were many people there with cameras, looking up and down the streets searching for Eva Perón. Skogskyrkogården

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Skogskyrkogården (The Woodland Cemetery) is located in the Enskededalen district south of central Stockholm, Sweden. It was designed in 1915 by architects Gunnar Asplund and Sigurd Lewerentz, winning a competition to build a new cemetery in the city. Walking through this cemetery, you feel like you have been transported to a beautiful forest with high trees. And unlike any cemetery I have ever visited, there is a cafe on site...

Bonaventure Cemetery

This large cemetery in Savannah, Georgia is perhaps best known as a central location in the celebrated novel Midnight in the Garden of Good and Evil, but it is also one of the city's largest and oldest public cemeteries, opening in 1846. It is located on the Wilmington River, east of downtown Savannah. It is a beautiful and tranquil space, with trees covered in spanish moss and beautiful, intricate marble tombs. Green-Wood Cemetery

Founded in 1838 and now a National Historic Landmark, Green-Wood was one of the first rural cemeteries in the United States. In addition to being a popular final resting place, it became of the most popular tourist attractions in New York state by the 1860s. According to our tour guide, families would gather at Green-Wood to go on tours, picnic, and relax in the green space. In fact, the popularity of Green-Wood cemetery led to the development of Central and Prospect Parks...
